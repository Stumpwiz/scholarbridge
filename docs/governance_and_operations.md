# Governance and Operations

## 1. Purpose of the System

ScholarBridge exists to help the Example Scholarship Committee manage donor and patron relationships with consistency, care, and historical awareness. It is intended to support committee work, not replace committee judgment. The system should make routine tasks easier, improve record clarity, and reduce dependence on any one person’s memory or spreadsheet habits.

## 2. Current Committee Workflow Realities

The committee’s current process is practical and spreadsheet-driven. It reflects years of real-world adaptation, including manual tracking, shared notes, and year-to-year carryover of institutional knowledge. ScholarBridge should respect this baseline and improve it gradually, rather than forcing abrupt changes in how committee members operate.

## 3. Annual Campaign Assumptions

Committee work follows recurring annual rhythms: planning outreach, contacting supporters, tracking responses, recording donations, and confirming acknowledgments. The system should support each campaign clearly while still allowing flexibility when timelines, priorities, or committee capacity shift.

## 4. Relationship Management

Solicitation Management is relational, not transactional. Records should support thoughtful, timely follow-up and respectful communication with supporters. The goal is to preserve context over time so the committee can engage consistently, understand prior interactions, and avoid repeated gaps or confusion.

## 5. Historical Continuity and Reporting

Historical records should remain understandable and usable across committee terms. Year-over-year visibility matters for planning, accountability, and donor solicitation management. Reporting should focus on clarity and continuity: what happened, what changed, and what follow-up is needed next.

## 6. Administrative Responsibilities

Operational ownership should be explicit. The committee should designate one or more administrators responsible for routine data oversight, process consistency, and handoff readiness. These responsibilities include maintaining naming conventions, reviewing major updates, and ensuring that records remain organized and complete.

## 7. User Access Expectations

Access should follow committee roles and practical need. Not every participant needs the same level of responsibility. Expectations should be clear: who can update records, who can review reporting, and who is accountable for final decisions on data corrections.

## 8. Backup and Continuity Considerations

Continuity planning should assume leadership transitions and occasional operational disruption. The committee should maintain a simple, repeatable backup routine and basic recovery instructions that future members can follow without specialized knowledge. Continuity materials should be kept current and easy to locate.

## 9. Long-Term Sustainability

ScholarBridge should remain lightweight, understandable, and maintainable over time. Sustainability depends on clear documentation, modest process discipline, and incremental improvement rather than large disruptive changes. The committee should prioritize reliability and usability over feature growth.

## 10. Guiding Principles

- Preserve institutional memory across years and leadership changes.
- Support evolving committee workflows without forcing rigid process rules.
- Avoid unnecessary complexity in process, language, and recordkeeping.
- Maintain data accuracy through clear ownership and regular review.
- Preserve historical donation and outreach records as a long-term asset.
- Support smooth transitions to future committee leadership.
- Keep the system approachable for non-technical users.
- Favor clarity, consistency, and solicitation management value over novelty.
