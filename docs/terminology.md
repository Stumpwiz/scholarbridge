# Terminology

Consistent language is required for long-term institutional continuity. These terms should be used in documentation, interface labels, and reporting unless formally revised.

## Preferred Terms

### Organization (preferred over Vendor)

Use **Organization** as the default institutional term. "Vendor" implies procurement or purchased services, which does not match the committee's stewardship relationship with supporters. "Organization" is neutral, accurate, and broad enough to include businesses, foundations, and community groups.

Historical references to the original vendor spreadsheet are acceptable when discussing legacy process origins.

### Donor

A person or organization that contributes funds or resources in support of scholarship outcomes.

### Patron

A supporter with an ongoing relational or advocacy dimension that may include, but is not limited to, financial contribution. In some cases, a patron is also a donor.

### Contact

A specific individual connected to an organization (or direct relationship) who is involved in communication, decisions, or follow-up.

### Campaign

A defined annual or seasonal stewardship period used to plan outreach, track responses, and report outcomes consistently.

### Solicitation

A campaign-bound operational record representing an organization participating in a campaign. A solicitation captures outreach actions, response status, requested and donated amounts, stewardship notes, acknowledgment progress, and assigned committee ownership.

## Distinction Summary

- Donor and patron describe relationship posture.
- Organization and contact describe structural records.
- Campaign defines reporting and operational time boundaries.
- Solicitation captures campaign-specific stewardship execution.
