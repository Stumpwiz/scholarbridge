# Vision

ScholarBridge exists to strengthen the Example Scholarship Committee's ability to manage donor and patron relationships over time. The system is evolving into a lightweight donor solicitation management CRM that helps the committee operate consistently year after year, even as membership changes.

## Operational Goals

- Maintain accurate and complete relationship records
- Support predictable annual solicitation and follow-up workflows
- Improve visibility into giving history and engagement status
- Reduce manual reconciliation work that currently lives across spreadsheets and email
- Preserve institutional memory so committee transitions are smoother

## Approach to Existing Practices

Current spreadsheet practices are not a failure mode; they are the committee's working baseline. ScholarBridge should improve workflow reliability and traceability without dismissing established habits that users trust.

Practical implication:

- Design import/export and familiar data views early
- Keep terminology aligned with committee language
- Avoid unnecessary complexity in early releases

## Guiding Principles

- Solicitation Management first: every record should support respectful, timely follow-up
- Historical continuity: prior campaigns must remain accessible and interpretable
- Simplicity: workflows should be clear enough for routine volunteer operation
- Incremental change: evolve from current process in controlled phases
